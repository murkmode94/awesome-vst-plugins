# Roland Hardware Emulation

This collection contains one of the most complete sets of Roland software emulations available, spanning five decades of Roland synthesizer history.

All titles below are part of the **Roland Cloud** / **Roland Software Synthesizer** ecosystem using Roland's **ACB (Analog Circuit Behavior)** or **ZEN-Core** modeling technologies.

---

## Flagship & Modeling Platform

- **ZENOLOGY** — Roland's ZEN-Core software synthesizer engine. Acts as a unified platform for Roland's entire ZEN-Core sound library. Compatible with patches from dozens of Roland hardware instruments.
- **ZENOLOGY FX** — ZEN-Core effects processor, providing Roland's onboard DSP effects as a plugin.
- **SOUND Canvas VA** — Software recreation of Roland's SC-88 / Sound Canvas GM2 sound module. 1,600+ sounds, 64-voice polyphony. The definitive General MIDI sound module emulation.

---

## Analog Polysynths

- **JUPITER-8** — 1981. Roland's masterpiece analog polysynth. Two oscillators per voice, dual VCF, and a character that defined synthesizer history.
- **JUPITER-4** — 1978. Roland's first polyphonic analog synthesizer. Simpler architecture, distinctive sound.
- **JUNO-106** — 1984. Roland's most popular analog polysynth. DCOs, built-in chorus, simple programming. Sounds incredible on pads and chords.
- **JUNO-60** — 1982. Predecessor to the 106. Warmer, slightly more organic character. Includes the classic JUNO-60 CHORUS emulation.
- **JX-3P** — 1983. Roland's DCO polysynth with PG-200 programmer support. Six voices, complex layering.
- **PROMARS** — 1979. Roland's preset-based string synthesizer/polysynth. Rare and distinctive.

## Monophonic & Bass Synthesizers

- **SH-101** — 1982. Classic monophonic analog synthesizer. Punchy bass, screaming leads, legendary in techno and electronic music.
- **SH-2** — 1979. Dual-oscillator monophonic synth. Thick, aggressive sound.
- **TB-303** — 1981. The Roland Bass Line. Foundation of acid house, techno, and electronic music broadly. The squelch is unmistakable.

## Digital & Hybrid Synthesizers

- **D-50** — 1987. Roland's Linear Arithmetic synthesis. Combined digital partials with PCM attack samples. Defined late-80s production sound.
- **JD-800** — 1991. Real-time programmable synthesizer with a knob for everything. Warm digital tones. Beloved by sound designers.
- **JV-1080** — 1994. 64-voice ROM-based expander. Massive, versatile sound library with SRX card expansion support.
- **XV-5080** — 2000. Roland's professional rackmount synthesizer module. 128-voice polyphony, 1GB sample ROM, full SRX expansion.

## System Series (ACB)

- **SYSTEM-8** — Analog circuit behavior polysynth. Doubles as a controller for SYSTEM-8 Plug-Out synths including Jupiter-8, Juno-106, etc.
- **SYSTEM-1** — PLUG-OUT compatible synthesizer. Morphing oscillators, color filter, ACB architecture.
- **SYSTEM-100** — 1975. Roland's semi-modular system. ACB recreation of a vintage patchable synthesizer.

## Drum Machines

- **TR-808** — 1980. The Roland Rhythm Composer. 808 kick, snare, hi-hats, and cowbell are embedded in hip-hop, electronic, and pop. Foundational.
- **TR-909** — 1983. The house and techno drum machine. Punchier, louder, and more aggressive than the 808.
- **TR-606** — 1981. Companion to the TB-303. Simple, characterful analog drum machine.
- **TR-707** — 1985. Digital drum machine with a distinct punchy character.
- **TR-727** — 1985. Percussion variant of the TR-707 with Latin and ethnic percussion voices.
